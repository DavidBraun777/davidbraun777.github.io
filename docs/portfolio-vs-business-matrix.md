# Portfolio vs Business Ownership Matrix

This matrix answers: "How much of each growth initiative belongs on
`dbraun.io` (portfolio) vs the business website (People's Connection LLC)?"

## Split by Initiative

| Initiative | Portfolio | Business | Why |
| --- | ---: | ---: | --- |
| 1) Conversion system (CTA, CRM, attribution, funnels) | 30% | 70% | Portfolio should route, business should convert and operate pipeline. |
| 2) Proof assets (quantified case studies) | 45% | 55% | Portfolio keeps concise trust proofs; business holds full commercial detail. |
| 3) Productized offers (packages, estimator, paid discovery) | 10% | 90% | Commercial offer design belongs primarily on business domain. |
| 4) Audience assets (lead magnet, newsletter, email automation) | 35% | 65% | Thought leadership can start on portfolio; monetized nurture belongs on business side. |
| 5) Distribution moat (SEO clusters + repurposed content) | 40% | 60% | Portfolio supports authority; business targets service-intent search demand. |
| 6) Operational buyer-readiness (P&L, SOPs, churn, dashboards) | 5% | 95% | Mostly business operations; portfolio only references high-level credibility stats. |

## Practical Rule

Use this rule when deciding where to ship a feature:

- If the page answers "Who is David and why trust him?" -> portfolio.
- If the page answers "What do you sell and how do I buy?" -> business.
- If both apply, publish summary on portfolio and full implementation on
  business, then cross-link both directions.

## Recommended Build Order

1. Business conversion stack (CRM + attribution + booking flow)
2. Business core service pages + productized offers
3. Case study set split across both sites (summary/full format)
4. SEO clusters and content repurposing loop
5. Newsletter + lifecycle automation
